# Day 6 - Business Insights

## Regional Performance
- Ibadan leads in total sales (₦394.7M), but Port Harcourt leads in profit (₦165.3M) despite lower sales, suggesting a better margin mix in Port Harcourt worth studying and replicating.
- Kano is the weakest region on both sales (₦377.3M) and profit (₦135.7M), and also carries a higher-than-average return rate (9%) - the clearest priority region for further investigation.

## Category Performance
- Furniture is the top category on both sales (₦629.8M) and profit (₦234.7M) - a strong candidate for increased marketing investment.
- Electronics is the weakest category by sales (₦378M) - worth investigating whether this is a pricing, competition, or awareness issue.

## Sales Rep Performance
- Actual sales far exceed rep targets across the board. This is unusual enough to suggest a data/target-setting methodology issue (e.g. targets may be set monthly while sales are tracked annually) rather than a genuine performance story - noted here as a caveat rather than a finding.
- Setting that aside, there's a real spread in output: top performer R07 (₦112.5M) outproduced the lowest, R20 (₦81.9M), by about 37% - worth understanding what separates them (territory, tenure, region).

## Customer Segments
- Consumer segment generates the most total revenue (₦698.3M), but this is driven by having the most customers (109), not higher value per customer - revenue per customer is nearly identical across all three segments (~₦6.4M each). Growth here is about acquiring more customers, not shifting segment focus.

## Shipping & Returns
- Return rate is identical (8%) across all three shipping methods - shipping method has no meaningful relationship to returns.
- Home category has a slightly elevated return rate (9%) versus other categories (8%) - a minor but worthwhile area to investigate further.
- Kano, Port Harcourt, and Abuja all show 9% return rates, while Ibadan and Lagos are lower at 7% - reinforces Kano (and to a lesser extent PH and Abuja) as needing closer attention.

## Trend
- Sales dipped through the year from a January high (₦183.4M) to a November low (₦142.8M), followed by a modest December rebound (₦166.4M) - a mild holiday-season lift rather than a dramatic seasonal spike.

## Overall
- Total Sales: ₦1,926,310,543
- Total Profit: ₦771,818,103 (~40% margin)
- Return Rate: 8.12%
